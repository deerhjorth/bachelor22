By Anna Mejlhede Jensen and Christian Hjorth

On January 30th, the world changed as WHO declared a new virus to be a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. COVID-19, as it came to be named, has since then affected almost every country worldwide. Following the recommendations proposed by WHO, governments in many countries began to enforce lockdowns in an attempt to slow down or possibly even stop the spread of the virus. These lockdowns hindered people in their everyday lives by closing down schools and businesses, and in some extreme cases, people were prohibited from leaving their homes to go out.

But how did this pandemic influence the lives of the citizens of different major cities around the world? This article explores how the mobility of different socioeconomic groups in different parts of the world was affected by the restrictions imposed by their governments.

Remember!

Wealth is relative! What may seem like a low income in one country can be considered a high or average income in another. We explore four major cities from low- and middle-income countries to look into the socioeconomical gap impacted by the COVID-19 restrictions.
