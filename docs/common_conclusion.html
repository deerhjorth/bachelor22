As the media's focus on the pandemic wanes, the long-term effects of the crisis continue to impact the world's population. To date, the pandemic has claimed the lives of over 6.6 million people and resulted in more than 650 million confirmed cases.

Low-income populations were disproportionately affected by the crisis. The loss of jobs, loved ones, and the ability to move freely has had a significant impact on their mental and financial well-being. A report published by the non-profit organization World Relief reveals that the pandemic pushed 97 million more people into poverty in 2020 alone, representing a setback of several years in the global effort to eradicate extreme poverty.

It is therefore essential for governments in low- and middle-income countries to reassess their lockdown measures, public transportation infrastructure, and job availability in order to address the high number of people living in poverty.
